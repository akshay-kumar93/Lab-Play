Materials Needed

  1. Food samples (e.g., potato, rice, bread, etc.)
  2. Iodine solution
  3. Dropper
  4. White tile or clean plate
  5. Knife (for cutting food samples)
  6. Water (optional, for diluting or preparing samples)

Procedure 🧪

  1. Prepare the Food Samples:

    🐭Cut the food items into small pieces if necessary.

  2. Place the Food Samples:

    🐭Arrange the food samples on a white tile or a clean plate. Ensure they are spaced out to avoid mixing.

  3. Apply the Iodine Solution:

    🐭Using a dropper, add a few drops of iodine solution to each food sample.

  4. Observe the Reaction:

    🐭Observe any color change in the food samples where the iodine solution was applied.

Expected Results

    😀Presence of Starch:

    If the food item contains starch, the iodine solution will change color from brown to a dark blue or black.

    😒Absence of Starch:

    If the food item does not contain starch, there will be no significant color change; the iodine solution will remain brown.

Explanation

    🤷‍♀️🤷 Starch reacts with iodine to form a blue-black complex, which is a clear indicator of the presence of starch in the food item.

Example Observations

  1. 🥔 Potato:

    🐭The iodine solution turns dark blue/black 🟤⚫, indicating a high starch content.

  2. 🍞 Bread:

    🐭The iodine solution turns dark blue/black 🟤⚫, indicating the presence of starch.

  3. 🍚Rice:

    🐭The iodine solution turns dark blue/black 🟤⚫, indicating the presence of starch.

  4. 🍎Apple

    🐭 Little to no color change 😒, indicating low or no starch content.

💀 Safety Precautions

    🐭Handle the iodine solution carefully, as it can stain clothes and skin.

    🐭Use a clean dropper for each food sample to avoid cross-contamination.

    🐭Wash your hands 🧼 after completing the experiment.
